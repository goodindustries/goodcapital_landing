For supporters investing in local preparedness.

Project Beehive is a flagship initiative of The Good Project, which also pursues operating partnerships and capital-structure work. This page summarizes philanthropic capital for Beehive—the hubs program—and how we think about endowment and network growth.

Why this needs patient capital

A Beehive is physical infrastructure: purchase and retrofit of a substantial facility, rotating inventory, training delivery, and command capability. That requires upfront investment—but the operating model is designed so retail, memberships, and endowment yield can reduce dependence on repeated crisis fundraising.

Initial fundraising target: $20M

We are raising toward an initial $20M allocation intended to cover:

  • The first Beehive in the Springfield, Missouri region (including ~$4.5M estimated setup: building, retrofit, and infrastructure).
  • A central endowment (roughly $8M of the raise) to generate recurring yield for long-term stability.
  • Operating runway for launch execution.
  • Reserve capital toward a second node.

Endowment approach (summary)

Endowment capital is intended to be managed with a yield-focused mandate—for example a mix of secured private credit, income-producing real estate, and liquid fixed income—with a modeled blended net yield in the 5–6% range. Returns are not legally guaranteed; they reflect an active strategy already used in affiliated operating experience, documented for partners during diligence—not passive index assumptions.

Illustrative Year 1 (Springfield case)

Combined revenue ~$1.05M

Operating cost ~$592K

Operating surplus (planning) ~$457K

Figures are planning assumptions; each future Beehive is modeled with the same framework adjusted for local conditions.

Who we are speaking to

We are building relationships with partners who fund institutions, not only one-off events:

  • Private foundations
  • Philanthropic high-net-worth individuals and families
  • Donor-advised funds
  • Institutional charitable capital

If you would like detailed materials, assumptions, or to schedule a conversation with leadership, we welcome inbound interest.

Expansion logic

After the flagship, we intend to add nodes across the region (e.g. Branson, Joplin, Bentonville, Tulsa, Kansas City, St. Louis)—keeping sites within a few hours’ drive for mutual aid and rapid deployment.
