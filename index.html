What is The Beehive?

A prepared people make a prepared city.

Buy a 72-hour kit, a water filter, a tent. Learn to can food, earn a first aid certification. Everything your family needs. Before you need it.

The Store
Everything your family needs before disaster strikes — 72-hour kits, water filtration, first aid, emergency power, and gear. All vetted, all in stock, all in your neighborhood. No shipping. No waiting.
Disaster School
Hands-on training in first aid, emergency response, search and rescue, and shelter-in-place. Taught by certified instructors. Earn credentials that matter when systems go down.
Shelter & Command
The Beehive doubles as a full disaster response command center — staffed, stocked, and ready to activate within hours of a declared emergency. When the grid goes down, this is where the city reports.
The Relief Team
Neighborhood volunteers trained to a professional standard — first aid certified, situationally aware, and pre-positioned in the community. They don't fly in. They live here.

The Organization

The Good Project

A nonprofit building the permanent community infrastructure America doesn't yet have.

Most relief organizations respond after disaster. We build before it. The Beehive is the first hub in a network designed to make every city in its region self-sufficient — stocked, trained, and ready before the warning goes out.

We are not a campaign. We are not a fund. We are an institution being built to last — governed by a board, funded by an endowment, and designed so that every hub generates the revenue to sustain itself and seed the next one.

The Beehive is our first proof.
