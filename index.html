01 · Why local command

Coordinating
the first 72 hours.

Money shows up after storms — neighbors need readiness before them. When the grid goes down, the Beehive’s operations room becomes neighborhood crisis command: live maps, logistics routing, resource coordination, and local leadership already in place before distant aid arrives.

03 · Shelter area

Safe beds before
the hotels fill up.

When evacuation orders go out, families need somewhere to go that night — not three days later. The Beehive’s shelter area provides cots, warmth, charging, and human presence from the first hours of a crisis.

Capacity Scalable cot layout · Partitioned areas
Amenities Power · Water · Sanitation · Warmth
