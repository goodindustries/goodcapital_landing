The Good Project
To relieve human suffering, in all forms, in all places.

Not after the disaster. Before it. The Beehive is the first hub in a network we're building city by city — each one stocked, trained, and ready. And each one designed to fund the next.

The Good Project exists to relieve human suffering, in all forms, in all places. To bring light and life, as Jesus does, to the inhabitants of the earth.

  1. All humans are children of God and therefore, regardless of circumstance, race, past actions, or social standing, are creatures of infinite worth.
  2. Given that infinite worth, suffering in any way and in any degree can and should be prevented, remedied, and, where possible, healed.
  3. Charity begins with Christlike love and ends in action. It includes bread, water, and the necessities of life, but does not exclude spiritual, social, mental, and other forms of support.
  4. The Savior Himself taught that "the poor ye have always with you" — no organization can fully rectify human suffering until His return.
  5. Yet any effort to provide relief or prosper any human being, according to the light-and-life pattern of Jesus Christ, is among the highest aims to which any organization can aspire.
"Verily I say, men should be anxiously engaged in a good cause, and do many things of their own free will, and bring to pass much righteousness."

The Good Project is one permutation of a good cause. One way to be anxiously engaged in doing good. We derive our four pillars from Jacob: to clothe the naked, feed the hungry, liberate the captive, and administer relief to the sick and afflicted.

How it works
Funded by generous local donors. Designed to sustain itself.

We are not a charity that asks indefinitely. The Beehive is built to generate its own revenue — through the store, the school, and disaster-relief reimbursements — so that each hub eventually pays for itself and seeds the next one.

We need capital to get the first one open. That's the ask.

The Store
Retail sales of emergency gear cover operating costs and generate margin.
Operation 61
Tuition-based training courses for individuals, families, and employers.
Shelter Reimbursements
FEMA and state contracts reimburse shelter operations during declared emergencies.
Donor Capital
Philanthropic gifts fund construction and startup costs for each new Beehive location.
